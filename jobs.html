Experiences with Job Applications

In my search for job opportunities, which require German, I initially reach out with a table to find out the necessary language requirements in both German and Swiss German.

While a few employers honestly state that they require a C1/C2 level in both languages, they also have the capability to use German as well. It comes down to their willingness to do so.

The majority of employers claim that Swiss German is not required. In response, I share my previous experiences and ask about how they address situations where coworkers do not care about others understanding the meetings and their performace and job is put at risk. I also ask whether they have policies against workplace discrimination and what measures they take.

The typical response? They often revise their requirements to "Swiss German C1/C2 is required". This change on the requirements indicates a preference to avoid responsibility for ensuring effective communication in the workplace.

What is funny is that some of that companies also require English. So the Swiss Logic here is:
- I don't like German, because it is not my mother tongue. So I won't use it
- But, I am perfectly fine using English, even though it is not my mother tongue
