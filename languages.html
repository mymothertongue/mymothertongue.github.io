National Languages

Whether Swiss German people like it or not, (High) German is the official German language of Switzerland.

- It is the one you learn in school.

- It is the one foreigners learn in language schools.

- It is the one I was required to speak, write, and understand to apply for the Swiss Citizenship.

And even the "interview" I had with the governmental institutions to get the Swiss Citizenship was held in (High) German.

I was never required to understand all Swiss Dialects at all levels

It is nice to understand them, sure, but it is nicer and legally obligatory to have respect above nacionalism, xenophobia and discrimination.

SWISS INTEGRATION POLICY

The fundamental principles of Swiss integration policy are outlined in the existing legal frameworks of the federal government, cantons, and municipalities. They can be summarized as follows:

- Integration is a mutual process, involving both the local and foreign populations.

- Integration requires openness from the resident population, a climate of recognition, and the removal of discriminatory barriers.

- The contribution of foreigners to integration is demonstrated through:

  • Respect for the fundamental values of the Federal Constitution,
  • Adherence to public safety and order,
  • A willingness to participate in economic life and acquire education, and

  • Knowledge of a national language.

- Integration is a core governmental responsibility, involving all levels of government in collaboration with social partners, non-governmental and foreign organizations, and other institutions.
